gear

While it's important to keep in mind that recordings are made by people rather than gear, having proper tools helps.

Schoeps CMT30FNU (Vintage)

With m934c omni/cardioid switchable and mk241c hypercardioid capsules. Commissioned by the ORTF in the 1960s. Astonishingly similar to current Schoeps mics but with a little more "blend".

Schoeps cmc5/cmc6

With mk5 omni/cardioid switchable, mk2 omni, mk21 wide cardioid, and mk8 figure 8 capsules. A worldwide standard for high-end orchestral recording.

RCA 44bx (Vintage)

The grandaddy of them all. Twice as old as I am, unrefurbished, and still sounds amazing. A classic Frank Sinatra and Elvis mic.

RCA BK-5B (Vintage)

Developed in the 1950s to withstand sound levels from close-range gunshots for film sound. Bombastic on bass drums.

AKG C12B (Vintage)

1960s tube mic with legendary handmade CK12 capsule.
